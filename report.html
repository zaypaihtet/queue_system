1. Introduction

In the competitive restaurant industry, customer experience is paramount to business success. One of the most significant factors affecting customer satisfaction is wait time management. Traditional queue management systems rely on manual processes, paper-based tickets, and rough estimates that often lead to customer frustration, operational inefficiencies, and lost revenue opportunities.

This project addresses the critical challenge of restaurant queue management by developing a modern, AI-assisted platform that streamlines the entire process from customer registration to seating. The system automates queue number generation, provides real-time status updates, predicts accurate wait times using artificial intelligence, and delivers comprehensive analytics for operational insights.

The Restaurant Queue Management System is designed to handle both dine-in (Table) and takeaway (Kitchen) queues simultaneously, allowing restaurant staff to manage different customer types efficiently. The platform leverages cutting-edge technologies including Flask web framework, JavaScript for dynamic user interactions, SQLite database for data persistence, and OpenAI's GPT-5 model for intelligent wait time predictions.

Key features of the system include:

  • Complete Customer Lifecycle Management: Full CRUD operations for adding, viewing, updating, and removing customers from the queue

  • Real-Time Updates: Live queue status updates ensuring staff always have current information
  • Search and Filter Capabilities: Quick customer lookup by name or phone number, with filtering by queue type
  • Analytics Dashboard: Comprehensive insights into queue performance, customer flow patterns, peak hours, and efficiency metrics
  • Responsive Design: Mobile-friendly interface accessible from tablets, smartphones, and desktop computers

2. Background

2.1 Problem Domain (Background of the Problem)

The restaurant industry faces significant challenges in queue management, particularly during peak hours when customer volume exceeds seating capacity. Traditional approaches to managing waiting customers are fraught with inefficiencies:

  • Manual Record Keeping: Staff members maintain handwritten lists or use basic spreadsheets, leading to errors, lost records, and difficulty in tracking customer status
  • Inaccurate Wait Time Estimates: Without data-driven insights, staff provide rough estimates based on intuition, often resulting in under or overestimation
  • Poor Customer Communication: Customers have no visibility into their queue position or realistic wait times, leading to frustration and abandonment
  • Lack of Data Analytics: Restaurants cannot analyze queue patterns, identify peak hours, or make data-driven decisions to optimize operations
  • Inefficient Resource Allocation: Without insights into queue dynamics, restaurants struggle to allocate staff and resources effectively

Research in restaurant operations management has shown that queue management significantly impacts customer satisfaction, retention rates, and overall revenue. According to industry studies, up to 30% of customers will leave if wait times exceed their expectations, and inaccurate wait time estimates are a primary cause of negative reviews and customer dissatisfaction.

2.2 Specific Problem

This project specifically addresses the following problems in restaurant queue management:

  1. Lack of Automation: Manual queue management is time-consuming, error-prone, and takes staff time away from customer service
  2. Inaccurate Wait Time Predictions: Simple calculations based on queue length fail to account for party size variations, time of day, service efficiency, and other critical factors
  3. No Real-Time Visibility: Both staff and customers lack real-time access to queue status and position updates
  4. Limited Analytics: Restaurants cannot track historical data, identify trends, or measure queue efficiency without a centralized system
  5. Poor Queue Type Differentiation: Mixing dine-in and takeaway customers in a single queue leads to confusion and suboptimal service
  6. Customer Frustration: Uncertainty about wait times and lack of transparency in the queuing process creates negative customer experiences

2.3 Disadvantages and Cons of the Problem

The existing problems in restaurant queue management result in several significant disadvantages:

Operational Disadvantages:

  • Staff Time Waste: Employees spend excessive time on manual queue tracking instead of customer service
  • Error Accumulation: Handwritten records lead to mistakes in customer information, queue numbers, and party sizes
  • Inefficient Table Turnover: Poor queue visibility leads to delays in seating customers when tables become available
  • Resource Misallocation: Without data on peak hours and queue patterns, staff scheduling is suboptimal

Customer Experience Disadvantages:

  • High Abandonment Rates: Customers leave when wait times are unclear or exceed expectations
  • Negative Reviews: Poor queue management directly correlates with negative online reviews and ratings
  • Customer Frustration: Lack of transparency and inaccurate estimates create frustration and dissatisfaction
  • Lost Loyalty: Poor experiences reduce customer retention and repeat visits

Business Disadvantages:

  • Revenue Loss: Customer abandonment during peak hours directly reduces revenue
  • Competitive Disadvantage: Restaurants with better queue management attract more customers
  • Missed Optimization Opportunities: Without analytics, restaurants cannot identify and address inefficiencies
  • Increased Operational Costs: Inefficient processes require more staff time and resources

3.6 Database Structure

Table: customers

Stores all customer queue records with AI prediction data.

Column Name Data Type Constraints Description
id INTEGER PRIMARY KEY, AUTOINCREMENT Unique customer record identifier
queue_number TEXT UNIQUE, NOT NULL Generated queue number (T001, K001, etc.)
customer_name TEXT NOT NULL Customer's name
phone TEXT NOT NULL Customer's phone number
party_size INTEGER NOT NULL Number of people in the party
queue_type TEXT NOT NULL Type: 'Table' or 'Takeaway'
status TEXT DEFAULT 'Waiting' Current status: 'Waiting', 'Seated', or 'Done'
estimated_wait INTEGER NOT NULL Estimated wait time in minutes
confidence INTEGER DEFAULT NULL AI prediction confidence (0-100)
ai_powered BOOLEAN DEFAULT FALSE Whether AI was used for prediction
ai_factors TEXT DEFAULT NULL JSON array of factors considered by AI
timestamp DATETIME DEFAULT CURRENT_TIMESTAMP Time customer joined queue
created_at DATETIME DEFAULT CURRENT_TIMESTAMP Record creation timestamp
updated_at DATETIME DEFAULT CURRENT_TIMESTAMP Last update timestamp

Table: analytics

Stores daily aggregated analytics data for performance tracking.

Column Name Data Type Constraints Description
id INTEGER PRIMARY KEY, AUTOINCREMENT Unique analytics record identifier
date DATE NOT NULL Date of analytics data
total_customers INTEGER DEFAULT 0 Total customers served that day
avg_wait_time REAL DEFAULT 0 Average wait time for the day
peak_hour TEXT DEFAULT NULL Busiest hour of the day
efficiency_score INTEGER DEFAULT 0 Queue efficiency rating (0-100)
hourly_data TEXT DEFAULT NULL JSON array of hourly customer counts
created_at DATETIME DEFAULT CURRENT_TIMESTAMP Record creation timestamp

5.3 Challenges Encountered and Solutions

Challenge 1: AI Prediction Accuracy

  • Issue: Initial predictions were too generic, not accounting for time-of-day variations
  • Solution: Enhanced context preparation to include peak hour detection, current queue composition, and historical patterns
  • Result: Improved prediction accuracy and user confidence in estimates

Challenge 2: Real-Time Updates

  • Issue: WebSocket implementation proved complex for initial development
  • Solution: Implemented JavaScript polling mechanism with 30-second intervals
  • Result: Simpler implementation providing adequate real-time feel, WebSocket planned for future

Challenge 3: Queue Number Generation

  • Issue: Needed unique, sequential numbers that reset daily but handle concurrent requests
  • Solution: Prefix-based system (T for Table, K for Kitchen) with database-driven increment logic
  • Result: Reliable unique number generation without race conditions
